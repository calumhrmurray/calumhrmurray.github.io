Observational cosmologist

I am a cosmologist working on weak gravitational lensing, counting the number of galaxy clusters in our Universe, the connection between galaxy shapes and large-scale structures, constraining modified gravity and many other topics. I am a member of the Euclid consortium, the DESC collaboration and the UNIONS collaboration.

calum.murray@cea.fr

Research interests

Weak gravitational lensing

Measuring how matter bends light, with applications to cluster masses, cosmic shear and future surveys.

Galaxy cluster cosmology

Using cluster abundance, mass calibration and survey selection effects to constrain cosmological models.

Intrinsic alignments

Studying the physical connection between galaxy shapes and the large-scale structure of the Universe.

Based at CosmoStat (CEA Saclay). Collaboration-friendly and always keen to talk lensing, clusters, and outreach.
