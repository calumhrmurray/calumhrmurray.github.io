Cosmologist • Weak lensing • Galaxy clusters

Making precise cosmology out of noisy skies

I study how galaxy clusters and weak gravitational lensing can sharpen our picture of the universe. My work blends simulations, likelihood design, and careful treatment of systematics so that surveys like Euclid and LSST can turn shear maps into robust measurements.

calum.murray@cea.fr

Current focus

Galaxy clusters

Mass calibration, abundance modelling, and the link between halos and cosmology in the low-noise limit.

Weak lensing methods

Filtering schemes and shear-field reconstruction that keep subtle signals intact while suppressing noise.

Inference & simulations

Simulation-based inference pipelines and likelihoods tuned to survey systematics for Euclid/LSST.

Projects on my desk

  • Euclid cluster mass calibration Building shear-based mass estimates that are resilient to projection effects and mis-centring.
  • Noise-aware reconstruction Testing filtering strategies that balance small-scale fidelity with stable large-scale modes.
  • Simulation-based likelihoods Exploring SBI approaches for cluster abundance, with transparent priors and survey-realistic mocks.
