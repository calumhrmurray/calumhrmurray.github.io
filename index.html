Making precise cosmology out of noisy skies

I'm an observational cosmologist working with large galaxy surveys. As projects like Euclid, DESI, and LSST get more precise, the big hurdle is no longer the data quality but how well we model the messy astrophysics that shapes what we observe. For years we could stick to large scales or fold complexities into nuisance parameters, but that shortcut is running out of road. Galaxy formation, intrinsic alignments, and selection effects are already limiting us, and for the next-generation surveys they will dominate the error budget. Pinning down dark energy—whether it's a simple cosmological constant or something dynamical—hinges on getting those models right.
